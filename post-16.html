UPSC Notes: Motion to Remove High Court Judge

July 25, 2025 Polity & Governance The Hindu

The Lok Sabha is set to take up a bipartisan motion for the removal of Justice Yashwant Varma of the Allahabad High Court, following a controversy involving cash recovery at his residence.

Key Aspects of the Motion:

  • Judge Concerned: Justice Yashwant Varma (currently Allahabad High Court, previously Delhi High Court).
  • Reason for Motion: Controversy erupted after half-burnt currency notes were found at his official Delhi residence during a fire on March 14.
  • Nature of Motion: Bipartisan, signed by 152 MPs from the ruling alliance and Opposition in Lok Sabha. A similar motion in Rajya Sabha (signed by 63 Opposition MPs) was not admitted.
  • Legal Framework: Proceedings are in line with the Judges (Inquiry) Act, 1968.
  • Initiation: Proceedings will first begin in the Lok Sabha, then move to the Rajya Sabha.
  • Inquiry Committee: Speaker Om Birla is expected to announce a three-member inquiry committee.
    • Composition: Chief Justice of India or a Supreme Court judge, a High Court Chief Justice, and a distinguished jurist.
  • Preceding Action: A Supreme Court committee had already inquired into the cash recovery, and its report was forwarded by the then Chief Justice of India to the President and Prime Minister.

Significance:

  • Highlights the accountability mechanism for judges in India.
  • Demonstrates parliamentary oversight in judicial matters.
  • Emphasizes adherence to the Judges (Inquiry) Act for due process.
