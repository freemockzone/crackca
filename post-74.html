Adopt Formalisation to Power Productivity Growth

Read Time: minutes

Context

  • India's formal manufacturing sector is witnessing a rising trend of informalisation via contractualisation.
  • This shift is affecting labour productivity, job quality, and long-term growth prospects.
  • The issue gains urgency as India seeks to become a global manufacturing hub.

Rising Contractualisation: The Trend

  • Contract labour share rose from 20% (1999-2000) to 40.7% (2022-23) in manufacturing sector.
  • This shift spans all industries â€” small, medium, and large enterprises.

Problems with Contractualisation

1. Exploitation of Workers

Contract workers often lack:

  • Job security
  • Core labour protections under Industrial Disputes Act, 1947
  • Bargaining power

2. Wage Disparities

Contract worker wages (2018-19):

  • 14.47% lower than regular workers
  • Gaps by enterprise size:
    • Large: 31% lower
    • Medium: 23%
    • Small: 12%
  • Employer labour cost for contract workers:
    • 24% lower on average
    • 50â€“85% lower in some industries

Impact on Productivity

Government Policy & Reform

1. Labour Code on Industrial Relations (2020)

Provisions:

  • Allows direct hiring on fixed-term contracts (no third party)
  • Mandates basic statutory benefits for non-permanent workers

Concerns:

  • May increase informalisation and weaken job quality if not implemented carefully

2. Policy Suggestions

  • Incentivise longer fixed-term contracts:
    • Subsidised social security contributions
    • Priority access to government skilling programs
  • Revive PMRPY (2016-2022):
    • Govt paid 12% EPF + EPS employer share
    • Benefited over 1 crore workers
    • Could reduce contract labour misuse and boost formal job creation

Way Forward

  • Promote formalisation over exploitative contractualisation.
  • Focus on stable, skill-rich employment for long-term productivity gains.
  • Align labour reforms with social protections, firm growth, and worker welfare.
  • Ensure data-driven policy monitoring through ASI and enterprise-level studies.
