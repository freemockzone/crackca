Calls grow to set up permanent National Commission for DNTs

Source: The Hindu Publication Date: August 3, 2025

üî∑ English Content:

Context: A national conference of Denotified, Nomadic, and Semi-Nomadic Tribes (DNTs) held in New Delhi saw growing demands for the establishment of a permanent national commission for these marginalized communities.

Who are DNTs?

  • Denotified Tribes (DNTs): Communities notified as ‚Äúcriminal tribes‚Äù under British-era Criminal Tribes Act of 1871, later *denotified* after independence.

  • Nomadic and Semi-Nomadic Tribes (NTs/SNTs): Traditionally migratory communities with non-sedentary lifestyles, often excluded from mainstream welfare schemes.

Key Developments at the Conference:

  • Organised by the Development and Welfare Board for DNTs, NTs, and SNTs.

  • Attended by BJP OBC Morcha chief and Rajya Sabha MP K. Laxman.

  • Community leaders: Presented a list of demands, led by the request for a permanent commission. Urged political advocacy in Parliament and executive support.

Historical Context ‚Äì Idate Commission (2015‚Äì2018):

  • Headed by Bhiku Ramji Idate.

  • Submitted report in 2018 recommending: Permanent national commission for DNTs. Classification and documentation of all DNT/NT/SNT communities. Special measures for development, education, livelihood, and legal protection.

Current Status:

  • No dedicated constitutional or statutory body for DNTs.

  • Existing welfare schemes often fail to reach these communities due to: Lack of documentation, fixed address, or community certificates. Overlap between SC/ST/OBC and DNTs leads to ambiguity in reservations.

Why a Permanent Commission?

  • To ensure: Focused policy formulation. Welfare implementation and grievance redressal. Institutional continuity and monitoring.

  • Parallel to other commissions like: National Commission for Scheduled Castes (NCSC). National Commission for Scheduled Tribes (NCST). National Commission for Backward Classes (NCBC).

Significance:

  • Would address decades of systemic marginalization.

  • Facilitate inclusive development, social justice, and constitutional guarantees.

  • Strengthens India's commitment to Sabka Saath, Sabka Vikas by reaching the most deprived.

Relevant UPSC Syllabus Linkages:

  • GS II: Welfare schemes for vulnerable sections; mechanisms, laws, institutions for protection and betterment of these sections.

  • GS I (Society): *Salient features of Indian society; diversity of India; role of marginalized communities.*

Potential UPSC Mains Questions:

  1. GS II: *‚ÄúDiscuss the challenges faced by Denotified, Nomadic, and Semi-Nomadic Tribes in accessing welfare schemes. Do you support the creation of a permanent commission for these communities?‚Äù*

  2. GS I: *‚ÄúThe historical stigmatization of DNTs continues to impact their socio-economic development. Analyze with examples.‚Äù*
