Theme: Industrial Output, Climate Disruptions, Economic Reporting

Weightage in IIP:

  • Mining + Electricity: 22.3%
  • Manufacturing: Remaining share

Impact of Monsoon on Output

Early and erratic monsoon → Waterlogging in key mining areas:

  • Odisha, Jharkhand, West Bengal
  • E.g. Ranchi’s Met Office: 504.8 mm rainfall (vs. normal 307 mm)

Disruptions in mining and power infra → Reduced supply → Lower output and power demand

Bright Spots in Industrial Output

Despite mining & electricity contraction:

  • Capital goods: +3.5%
  • Intermediate goods: +5.5%
  • Infrastructure goods: +7.2%
  • Inference: Growth driven by government infrastructure spending.

Climate Risk & Economic Reporting in India

Problem:

Official economic data (IIP, GDP, etc.) rarely mention climate-related disruptions.

Attribution often blamed on:

  • High base effect
  • Supply chain issues
  • Global/domestic demand fluctuations
  • Input cost spikes

Missed Acknowledgment:

Climate impact on mining, transport, electricity is underplayed or ignored.

Climate attribution = complex + politically sensitive.

Global Best Practices

Institutions like:

  • European Central Bank
  • Bank of England

Have begun climate-risk mapping to financial and output data.

RBI now includes climate risk in its Financial Stability Reports, but not in IIP/GDP metrics yet.

Takeaway

India must move towards institutionalising climate attribution in economic reporting, especially as the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events increase. Acknowledging this openly is key to building resilient and future-proof economic systems.
