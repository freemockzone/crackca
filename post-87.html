Police Reforms & Custodial Torture in India

Key Issue:

The persistence of custodial torture and the urgent need for a shift from coercive policing (Dirty Harry model) to evidence-based, professional methods (Sherlock Holmes model).

Context:

  • Ajith Kumar's Custodial Death in Tamil Nadu (2025) reignites debate on police torture.
  • Contrasts between two policing models:
    • üïµÔ∏è‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏è Sherlock Holmes: Evidence-driven, logical, lawful.
    • üî´ Dirty Harry: Coercive, fast, rule-breaking.

  • Total: 687 deaths (‚âà2-3 per week)

    • Gujarat (81)

Why Torture Persists:

  • Lack of training & oversight
  • Pressure for quick results
  • Poor infrastructure
  • Societal tolerance & normalization
  • Weak accountability: Rare disciplinary or criminal action
  • Targeted groups: Dalits, tribals, migrants, slum-dwellers

Legal & Institutional Gaps:

  • D.K. Basu vs State of West Bengal (1996): Safeguards against custodial violence
  • Puttaswamy Case (2017): Emphasized right to dignity
  • Law Commission 273rd Report (2017): Called for standalone anti-torture law
  • Status:
    • No anti-torture law yet
    • UN Convention Against Torture not ratified
    • India ranked ‚Äúhigh-risk‚Äù in Global Torture Index (2025)

Scientific & Global Evidence Against Torture:

‚ùå Torture is ineffective:

  • Shane O'Mara (2015): Torture impairs memory, leads to false confessions
  • Algerian War, CIA black sites: Intelligence gained was mostly useless
  • Innocence Project (USA): 375+ wrongful convictions overturned due to false, coerced confessions

‚úÖ What works:

  • PEACE Model (UK):
    • Preparation, Engagement, Open-ended questioning
    • Result: Fewer false confessions, better public trust
  • Norway (Anders Breivik), USA (Zazi): Respectful, non-coercive interrogation led to full cooperation

üß† HIG (USA):

  • Peer-reviewed studies: Non-coercive techniques more reliable and timely

Recommendations:

  • 1. Ratify UN Convention Against Torture
  • 2. Enact a standalone Anti-Torture Law
  • 3. Adopt & institutionalize the PEACE model nationwide
  • 4. Improve training & infrastructure
  • 5. Ensure strict accountability & zero-tolerance for custodial abuse

Ethical and Constitutional Perspective:

  • Torture violates Article 21 (Right to life and dignity)
  • Democracy test: State must protect the weak, not brutalize them
  • Every instance of torture = ‚Äúa stain on the soul of the State‚Äù
