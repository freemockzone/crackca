ICJ Advisory Opinion on Climate Obligations

  • Obligations of states to reduce anthropogenic greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions.
  • Legal consequences for failure to meet these obligations.

Positive Takeaways for the Global South:

1. Recognition of the Full Climate Regime:

  • Emphasised the continuity of the UNFCCC, Kyoto Protocol, and Paris Agreement.
  • Countered the developed nations' stance of sidelining the Kyoto Protocol.
  • Reinforced climate finance, tech transfer, and capacity building obligations of developed countries (Annex-I & II of UNFCCC).

2. Reaffirmation of CBDR-RC Principle:

  • Common But Differentiated Responsibilities and Respective Capabilities (CBDR-RC) identified as the ‚Äúcore guiding principle‚Äù for implementation of climate treaties.
  • Recognized its application beyond climate treaties, e.g., to biodiversity agreements.

3. Acknowledgement of Dynamic Development Status:

  • Noted the Paris Agreement‚Äôs qualification ‚Äúin light of national circumstances‚Äù.
  • Signaled that development categories are not fixed, inviting future debate.

On Enforceability and Legal Obligations:

Nature of Obligations:

  • Domestic mitigation and support = Obligations of conduct, not result.
  • Only procedural commitments (e.g., NDC submissions) = stronger obligations.
  • Enforcement depends on:
  • Jurisdiction of appropriate courts.
  • Case-specific conditions.

Practical Impact:

  • Reaffirms Paris principles amid developed countries‚Äô inertia.
  • Breaks no new ground on compliance or enforcement.

Gaps and Shortcomings:

1. Neglect of Developmental Imperatives:

  • Overlooks the energy-poverty-growth dilemma of the Global South.
  • Ignores that low-carbon pathways require massive finance and tech access.

2. Equity Treated Merely as a Guiding Principle:

  • Equity and CBDR-RC declared to guide interpretation, not create new obligations.

3. Litigation vs. Negotiation:

  • Enthusiasm in civil society for national/regional litigation.
  • For global negotiations, impact likely limited.
