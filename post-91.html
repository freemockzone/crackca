Bihar Electoral Roll Revision (2025) – Special Intensive Revision (SIR)

Published on August 1, 2025

1. Context

  • Bihar is the first state where the Election Commission (EC) is implementing a Special Intensive Revision (SIR) of electoral rolls, ahead of Assembly elections due by November 2025.
  • EC ordered fresh preparation of electoral rolls (unlike routine revisions), requiring all 7.89 crore electors to submit new enumeration forms by July 25, 2025.

2. Key Objectives of SIR

  • To update and clean electoral rolls.
  • To ensure inclusion of eligible citizens and removal of ineligible entries (duplicates, deceased, untraceable, permanently shifted).

3. Current Developments

  • 7.23 crore forms received out of 7.89 crore.
  • Around 65 lakh names set to be removed:
    • 22 lakh deceased
    • 36 lakh permanently shifted/untraceable
    • 7 lakh duplicate registrations

4. Document Verification Measures

  • Minority of applicants submitted eligibility documents → EC planning supportive measures:
    • Deployment of AEROs (Assistant Electoral Registration Officers) to use existing databases to verify eligibility.
    • Use of:
      • Vikas Register (Mahadalit Vikas Mission) → caste certificates.
      • Khatiyan (land records) → residence/identity proof.
  • Camps to be held at Block Development Officers' (BDO) offices to assist in documentation.
  • EC to deploy **volunteers** for helping citizens.

5. Eligibility Criteria & Proofs

  • Year 2003 designated as “probative evidence of eligibility”:
    • Electors on 2003 roll presumed citizens.
    • Their children can submit 2003 extract for parents, but **must submit own documents**.
  • 11 indicative documents accepted (non-exhaustive list):
    • Birth certificate, caste certificate, passport, education certificate, family register, land/house allotment certificate, etc.
    • Residence certificate not on EC’s list but sought by BLOs in practice.
  • EC rejected Aadhaar, ration card, and Voter ID as valid proof (matter in Supreme Court, hearing on Aug 12–13, 2025).

6. Timeline

  • July 25: Last date for submitting forms.
  • August 1 – September 1: Period for **claims and objections**:
    • Add missing names.
    • Remove ineligible names.
    • Correct entries.
  • September 30: Final electoral roll to be **published**.

7. EC Communication

  • CEC Gyanesh Kumar: Announced draft roll publication on August 1, available online and with recognised political parties.
  • Encouraged citizens and political parties to participate in claims & objections process.

8. Legal & Political Angle

  • SIR order challenged in Supreme Court by activists & Opposition.
  • Key concerns:
    • Omission of widely used IDs (e.g., Aadhaar, Voter ID).
    • Potential disenfranchisement of genuine voters.

9. Relevance for UPSC

    • Voter enrolment process and electoral roll management.
    • Citizenship verification in electoral processes.
  • GS Paper II – Issues in federalism and centre–state relations (state-specific revision led by central agency).

Key Terms to Remember

  • SIR: Special Intensive Revision
  • ERO/AERO: Electoral Registration Officer / Assistant Electoral Registration Officer
  • BLO: Booth Level Officer
  • Khatiyan: Land ownership record
  • Vikas Register: Household-level SC data (Bihar Mahadalit Vikas Mission)
