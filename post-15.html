UPSC Notes: India-UK Trade Pact – A Template for Future Deals

July 25, 2025 Economy & International Relations Indian Express

India and the UK have signed the Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement (CETA), aiming to significantly boost bilateral trade and deepen engagement across various sectors.

Key Features & Benefits:

  • Goal: Double bilateral trade from ~$56 billion.
  • UK-India Vision 2035: Envisages deeper ties in defence, tech, education.
  • India's Tariff Reduction: India to substantially lower tariffs (average on British products to fall from 15% to 3%). This signals India's willingness to integrate with developed countries.
  • Duty-Free Access for India: "Unprecedented duty-free access for 99% of India’s exports to the UK."
    • Covers labour-intensive sectors: textiles, leather, footwear, gems & jewellery.
    • Includes engineering goods and auto components.
  • Services & Labour Mobility: Significant commitments for Indian professionals.
    • Increased market access in IT, IT-enabled services, financial, legal, professional, and educational services.
    • Double contribution convention agreement: Helps Indian professionals avoid social security payments in UK, reducing business costs.

Significance & Future Implications:

  • Global Context: Comes amidst global trade shifts due to US tariff policies.
  • Template for Future Deals: Could serve as a template for India's ongoing negotiations with the US and EU, especially for contentious issues.
  • Integration with Global Supply Chains: Delhi's push for such agreements is crucial for closer integration with global supply chains.
