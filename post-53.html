UPSC CSE Notes: Criminalising Adolescent Sex & POCSO Act Reforms

Monday, July 28, 2025
~3 Minute Read

üßæ Context:

  • POCSO Act, 2012: Enacted to protect children (<18 years) from sexual offences.
  • Issue: Consensual sex among adolescents (16-18 years) is criminalised under POCSO.

üßë‚Äç‚öñÔ∏è Judicial Interventions & Submissions:

  • Indira Jaising (Amicus Curiae):
    • Filed submission in SC in Nipun Saxena case.
    • Argued: Consensual sex between 16-18-year-olds should not be criminalised.
    • Proposed reading an exception into:
      • POCSO Act
      • Section 63 of Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS)
    • Objective: Prevent misuse of law; preserve its protective intent.
  • Madras HC (2021) - Vijayalakshmi vs State Rep:
    • Suggested caveat: Age difference between partners should be ‚â§ 5 years.
    • Prevents exploitation by significantly older individuals.

üìë Law Commission (2023) Recommendations:

  • Opposed reducing age of consent from 18 to 16.
  • Recommended: Use of guided judicial discretion in sentencing for consensual relationships among 16‚Äì18-year-olds.

‚öñÔ∏è Legal Framework:

  • Under POCSO, a person <18 is a child; consent irrelevant.
  • Relevant Sections:
    • Section 6, POCSO: Punishment for penetrative assault.
    • Section 9, Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006
    • IPC & BNS provisions on sexual offences.

‚ö†Ô∏è Concerns with Current Law:

  • Criminalises normal adolescent behaviour.
  • Leads to misuse against consensual, non-exploitative relationships.
  • Ignores psychosexual maturity (often reached by age 16).

‚úÖ Suggested Reforms:

  • Legal exception for consensual sex between 16‚Äì18-year-olds.
  • Ensure non-exploitative relationships only.
  • Strengthen sexual education & legal awareness among adolescents.

üß† Takeaway for UPSC Aspirants:

  • Understand intersection of law, rights & societal norms.
  • Reforms must balance child protection with adolescent autonomy.
  • Possible area for Essay, GS-II (Governance, Rights Issues), or Ethics Case Study.
